Quell Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 13, 2026 · Last updated: July 13, 2026

Short version: Quell does not collect, sell, or transmit your browsing history. Filtering runs on your device. Settings stay in your browser’s local storage.

Who we are

Quell is an open-source browser extension that blocks ads and trackers using Declarative Net Request (Manifest V3) and local cosmetic/scriptlet filters. Source code: github.com/AndreasNE89/quell.

Data we collect

We do not collect personal data. Quell does not operate analytics, crash reporting, advertising identifiers, or remote logging.

  • Browsing activity: Not sent to us. Network blocking is applied by Chrome’s Declarative Net Request engine using rules shipped in the extension package (and optional updates you enable).
  • Settings & allowlist: Stored locally via chrome.storage on your device. Not uploaded.
  • Statistics: Any on-device counters (e.g. badge hints) stay local when available. Quell does not sync them to a server.

Permissions

Quell requests only what is needed to block ads/trackers:

  • declarativeNetRequest — apply filter rules without reading page content on the network path.
  • scripting — inject cosmetic filters and approved scriptlets on pages you visit.
  • storage — save your preferences and site allowlist.
  • webNavigation — know when navigations occur so filters can be applied consistently.
  • <all_urls> host access — required so blocking and cosmetics can work on websites generally (the purpose of an ad blocker).

Third parties

Filter lists (e.g. EasyList and related community lists) may be downloaded from their publishers when you update lists. Those downloads are list files only; Quell does not send your browsing history to list publishers as part of filtering.

We do not sell or share user data with third parties because we do not collect it.

Children

Quell is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect children’s data.

Changes

We may update this policy when the extension’s data practices change. The effective date above will be revised accordingly.

Contact

Questions about privacy: open an issue on GitHub Issues or contact the maintainer via the repository.
